UCLA Campus Climate Survey

Thank you for being part of this year's senior survey. Your experiences help UCLA understand how students are doing — and where we can do better.
  • Time: About 8–12 minutes.
  • Sections: Belonging, expression climate, peer environment, experiences of bias, reporting, self-censorship, and a few demographic questions at the end.
  • Optional questions: Most questions are optional. You can choose "Prefer not to answer" or skip where allowed.
Confidentiality. Your individual responses are confidential. Results are reported only in aggregate, and subgroups with fewer than 10 respondents are not separately reported.

Option A

Full topic × context matrix

40 cells (8 topics × 5 contexts) · ~3–4 min · Richest data, highest burden

Contexts: in class · faculty outside class · peers · campus events · online
Scale: 1 → 4 + N/A  ·  Time: ~3–4 min

Analyze: Topic × context interactions; benchmarks directly against FIRE rankings (5-context structure).

Trade-off: Richest data, but real fatigue / straight-lining risk on this item alone.

Option B recommended

Lighter matrix — 3 actionable contexts

24 cells (8 topics × 3 contexts) · ~2 min · Best balance for general survey

Contexts: in class / academic · peers outside class · faculty 1-on-1
Scale: 1 → 4 + N/A  ·  Time: ~2 min

Analyze: Maps to UCLA's three intervention levers (curriculum, peer culture, mentorship); cross-tabs cleanly with Q5.

Trade-off: Manageable burden, but drops online & public-event contexts.

Option C

Academic vs. social split

16 cells (8 topics × 2 settings) · ~1.5 min · Simplest, loses granularity

Settings: academic (class, sections, office hours) · social (peers, dorms, clubs, events)
Scale: 1 → 4 + N/A  ·  Time: ~1.5 min

Analyze: Broad academic vs. social only — can't drill into specific contexts within each bucket.

Trade-off: Simplest to answer & analyze, but loses within-bucket granularity (large lecture vs. seminar; dorm vs. club).

Decision needed from leadership
  1. Split Q6 into Q6a (treatment of identity groups) and Q6b (treatment of expressed viewpoints), or keep as a single ambiguous item?
  2. If split, ask about treatment "by other students" only, or also "by faculty/staff"?
The current Q6 conflates identity-based and viewpoint-based treatment. Respondents will resolve the ambiguity inconsistently, which makes the data hard to interpret. Splitting adds two items to the survey but separates two distinct constructs.

Section 5 of 7

Bias, harassment, or discrimination

Your experiences and observations at UCLA.

7During your time at UCLA, did you personally experience or observe bias, harassment, or discrimination directed at someone because of their identity or background (such as race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability, socioeconomic background, political beliefs, or age)?
For this question, "bias, harassment, or discrimination" refers to actions, comments, or treatment directed at someone because of their identity or background. It does not include general disagreements, dissatisfaction with academic outcomes such as grades, or interpersonal conflicts unrelated to identity.
Follow-up — shown only if you selected Experienced / Observed / Both

7d. If you are comfortable, briefly describe what happened. (Optional)

If you have experienced harm and would like support, UCLA resources are available at [insert link — CARE, Office of Equity, Diversity & Inclusion, Counseling and Psychological Services].

Decision needed from leadership
  1. How detailed should Section 5 bias follow-up items be — keep the current 7a–7d structure, or expand to a HEDS / AAU-style behavioral checklist?
  2. Add a mid-survey wellness / resource-awareness check-in? (Suggested owner: Danielle)
More depth gives richer data but increases respondent burden and may broaden IRB scope. Mid-survey check-in addresses concerns about whether students know what support resources exist before they're prompted to disclose harm.

Section 7 of 7

Sharing your views

How often you held back from sharing your views.

Decision needed from leadership
  1. Add political ideology and religious affiliation as end-of-survey demographic items (D15–D18)?
Without these, Q10 and Q11 produce uninterpretable headline numbers — we cannot say which students self-censor, only that some do. Adding them is convention across FIRE, Heterodox Academy CES, and HERI DLE. Placement at the end of the survey (not adjacent to Q10/Q11) avoids priming effects.
10I felt hesitant to share my political views on campus.
11I felt hesitant to share my religious beliefs on campus.
